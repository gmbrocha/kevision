Current goal:
Train CloudHammer into a reliable cloud detector/cropper. The target is one
real clouded revision area per crop, with false positives beaten down through
reviewed hard negatives, held-out evals, and model training. Pipeline or review
tool work only matters if it directly creates training data, eval coverage, or
model-facing feedback for that goal.

Do not drift:
Marker anchors, split/merge repair logic, texture/text/symbol false positives,
and other pipeline rules are lessons to bake into the model/training/eval loop,
not a reason to spend forever polishing plumbing.

Next major goal:
After the cloud crops are reliably good, move to reliable itemized/detail
extraction from those crops: read the scope/details inside and immediately
around the accepted cloud, preserve applicable detail callouts/items, and turn
that into useful reviewable deliverable rows.

Review bias guardrail:
Dense plan context is not automatically partial, overmerged, or false. If the
candidate actually captures one intended clouded revision area, mark it accept
even when the surrounding drawing content is visually brutal. Use partial only
when the real cloud is clipped/missed; use overmerged only when distinct clouds
are bundled together.

Training guardrail:
Accepting a crop does not mean the full green crop rectangle becomes model
truth. Door swing arcs, fixture circles, text curves, seals, and plan geometry
stay non-cloud. Training boxes stay on the actual revision-cloud motif only.
