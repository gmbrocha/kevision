# Independent Contractor Agreement — DRAFT

> Fill in the `[BRACKETS]` before sending. Have them sign before submitting first invoice.
> This is a starting point, not legal advice.

---

**INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR AGREEMENT**

This Independent Contractor Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of **[DATE]**, between:

**Client:** [COMPANY LEGAL NAME], a [state] corporation ("Client")
**Contractor:** Glen Brochard, an individual ("Contractor")

---

## 1. Services

Contractor agrees to perform the following services ("Services"):

Development of a drawing revision automation tool ("kevision"), including but not limited to computer vision processing, revision detection, aggregation pipeline, and related tooling as directed by Client.

Scope may be adjusted by mutual written agreement (email is sufficient).

---

## 2. Compensation

**Hourly Rate:** $[YOUR RATE] per hour

**API / Infrastructure Costs:** Contractor will pass through actual third-party API costs (e.g. AI model inference) at cost with no markup. Contractor will provide documentation of costs with each invoice.

**Invoicing:** Contractor will invoice Client [weekly / bi-weekly / monthly — pick one]. Payment is due within **[15 or 30] days** of invoice receipt.

**Method:** [Check / ACH / wire / whatever they use]

---

## 3. Independent Contractor Status

Contractor is an independent contractor, not an employee of Client. Contractor is responsible for all taxes on compensation received. Client will not withhold taxes, provide benefits, or cover workers' compensation.

---

## 4. Intellectual Property

All work product, code, documentation, and deliverables produced by Contractor under this Agreement are considered work made for hire and are the sole property of Client upon receipt of full payment.

Contractor retains no rights to the work product upon project completion or termination.

---

## 5. Confidentiality

Contractor agrees to keep confidential all non-public information shared by Client in connection with this Agreement, including but not limited to source code, business data, internal processes, and project specifications. This obligation survives termination of the Agreement.

---

## 6. Term and Termination

This Agreement begins on the date above and continues until terminated. Either party may terminate with **[7 or 14] days** written notice. Client is responsible for payment of all hours worked and costs incurred up to the termination date.

---

## 7. Limitation of Liability

Contractor's total liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid in the **3 months** prior to the claim.

---

## 8. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of **[Mississippi / Client's state — whichever you agree on]**.
